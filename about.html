About Origami

Origami is the art of paper-folding. Its name derives from Japanese words ori (“folding”) and kami (“paper”). Traditional origami consists of folding a single sheet of square paper (often with a colored side) into a sculpture without cutting, gluing, taping, or even marking it.

About Origami Artistry

An origamist or an origamian is a person who is associated with the art of origami. Variants of traditional origami include modular and action origami, Golden Venture, wet folding, pureland, strip folding, tessellations and kusudama. With origami, you're only limited by your imagination.
